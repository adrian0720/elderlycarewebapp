Pulse Monitoring in Aged Persons

Regular pulse monitoring is essential for older adults to manage heart health and detect potential issues early.

Heart Health

A normal pulse can indicate good heart health, but changes may suggest underlying issues.

Dehydration Warning

An elevated pulse may indicate dehydration, which is common in older adults.

Poor Circulation

A low pulse may signal poor circulation or cardiovascular issues.

It is important to monitor pulse regularly for early detection of these issues.

High Pulse Rate

Increased heart rate could indicate stress or cardiovascular disease. Regular monitoring is crucial.

Medication Effect

Some medications can affect pulse rates. It's important to track changes when on specific drugs.

Blood Pressure Relation

Pulse rate is often related to blood pressure, so monitoring both is necessary for older adults.

Normal Pulse Rate

The normal pulse rate for adults is typically between 60 and 100 beats per minute. It may vary based on factors like age, health, and fitness level.

Oxygen Level

Oxygen saturation levels are typically measured with a pulse oximeter. A normal oxygen level is 95% or higher. Lower levels may indicate respiratory issues.

About Us

Our website is an integral part of our capstone project, designed to improve elderly care at Anawim. Our mission is to use cutting-edge technology to create solutions that enhance the safety, health, and overall well-being of elderly individuals. We believe that technology can play a vital role in transforming traditional care methods into more efficient and effective practices, providing our users with peace of mind and the highest level of support.

Our dedicated team comprises experts from various fields who are passionate about making a positive impact. Cedrick Abad, our ESP Programmer, is responsible for integrating the ESP32 hardware components, allowing real-time monitoring and data collection. Jan Michael Sulit, our Website and Desktop Application Programmer, ensures the system’s seamless operation, creating a user-friendly interface that promotes accessibility and ease of use for both caregivers and elderly users.

Adrian Palmes, our MIT Programmer, focuses on implementing accurate algorithms and medical technology components that are essential for monitoring vital signs and other health indicators. With his expertise, we are able to provide reliable and precise data that caregivers and medical professionals can use to make informed decisions. Our Documentation Specialist, Nitzan Mariano, ensures that every step of our project is well-documented, from system designs to implementation processes, making it easier for future developments and evaluations.

Lenard Bengco, our Research Specialist, plays a crucial role in ensuring that our solutions are tailored to the unique needs of Anawim’s elderly community. By conducting thorough research on elderly care trends and technological advancements, Lenard helps our team align our project with the best practices and innovative solutions in the field. Together, our team's collaborative efforts and expertise are aimed at making a difference and contributing to the welfare of the elderly in our community.
